What We Test For

Pb 14

Lead

One of the scarier contaminants to show up when testing drinking water. There are many cases where lead gets into a households drinking water due to the corrosion of the cities water pipes. For adults, lead in drinking water can lead to kidney problems and high blood pressure in adults (among other things). For children and infants the effects are much more noticeable. Lead in drinking water can lead to physical and/or mental development delays.

Coli

Bacteria (Coliform)

While coliform bacteria itself is unlikely to do your body any harm, the presense of them in your water may indicate more harmful organisms are contaminating your water.

Ca 20

Calcium

Along with magnesium, calcium is the most common mineral that makes your drinking water hard. Calcium in your water can lead to a buildup in your pipes, which can corrode them and lead to worse contaminants leaking into your drinking water, along with contributing to the breakdown of household appliances.

NO3-

Nitrates

Excessive nitrates in your drinking water can lead to serious illness, shortness of breath, and even “blue-baby syndrome” for infants. Also, the discovery of nitrates helps indicate the presence of more serious contaminants, such as bacteria or pesticides. Whether through leaking septic tanks, runoff from fertilizer, or the erosion of natural deposits, there is no shortage of how nitrates can enter your drinking water.

NO2-

Nitrites

Similar to nitrates, nitrites in your water can be extremely dangerous for infants. Infants who drink water with a large amount of nitrites can lead to them developing “blue-baby syndrome” or other serious illnesses.

pH

pH

Determines whether your water is hard or soft. Soft water tends to have a higher level of toxic metals, which can lead to health issues associated with the metals found in them. Hard water, while not directly responsible for serious health problems, can corrode your pipes, allowing more dangerous contaminants to enter the drinking water.

Turb

Turbidity

Measures the cloudiness of your drinking water. Higher levels of turbidity can indicate that there are other microorganizms in the water (bacteria, parasites) that can contribute to nasusea, cramps, diarrhea and headaches.

Alka

Akalinity

Alkalinity is the “measure of the ability of your water to resist changes in pH”. In other words, a low level of alkalinity means the pH of your water will vary greatly between acidic and basic over time.

EColi

Bacteria (E. Coli)

The presence of E. coli in drinking water can contribute towards diarrhea, nausea, stomach cramps, and vomiting. Discovering E. coli in water can also be a main indicator of other, more harmful microbes existing in the water itself.

Cl-

Chloride

Chloride is naturally found in water and is generally considered to not be harmful when found in drinking water. However, chloride may be responsible for causing the corrosion of metal pipes which can lead to more harmful contaminants getting into the drinking water. If present in water, it may give the water a saltier taste.

Cu 29

Copper

While having some Copper in your diet is essential for human life, too much copper can lead to health issues. A small exposure to excessive amounts of copper can contribute to nausea, abdominal pain, diarrhea, and vomiting. A large exposure to copper above the recommended dosage set by the EPA can lead to much more severe health issues like liver damage and kidney disease.

F-

Flouride

In many parts of the country Fluoride gets added to the drinking water to help prevent tooth decay. While small amounts of fluoride can be beneficial, long term exposure to fluoride in excess of the EPA’s recommended dose can lead to skeletal fluorosis in adults or dental fluorosis in children.

Hard

Hardness

The World Health Organization states there doesn’t appear to be any direct correlation between your drinking water and health effects. The hardness of your water is determined mainly determined by the amount of calcium and magnesium in the water. Excessive amounts of calcium and magnesium can corrode pipes and lead to more harmful contaminants leaking into your water.

Fe 26

Iron

While iron in your drinking water will rarely result in health issues, the iron sediments may contain harmful bacteria. The amount of iron in the water can leave a poor taste in your mouth or even leave brownish stains on clothes or appliances around the house.

Mn 25

Manganese

Low doses of manganese found in water is generally safe, however a high exposure has been associated with effecting the nervous system and resulting in a syndrome similar to parkinson disease.

Mg 12

Magnesium

Similar to calcium, magnesium is associated with being one of the two most common minerals that help determine the hardness of water. While drinking hard water has never been proven to have any negative side effects, the hard water itself can cause corrosion of your household pipes and lead to more dangerous contaminants entering your water.

Pest

Pesticides

Prolonged exposure to specific types of pesticides in drinking water can lead to cancer, cause birth defects to new borns, and even effect the central nervous system.
